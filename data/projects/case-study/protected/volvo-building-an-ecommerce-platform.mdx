## Project Brief

We were commissioned by Volvo to design a new e-commerce website that would allow their workshop partners to search their directory and purchase genuine parts. Mechanics would be able to log in, enter a vehicle's registration plate, and find the correct parts for that specific car.

## The Team

Volvo commissioned the design agency SQLI to lead the design and UX efforts, while Capgemini was tasked with the development of the site.

There were three main parties involved; Volvo, SQLI and Capgemini, but Volvo also had several departments to consider (logistics, business and etc). As the Lead UX Designer, I was responsible for managing and delegating work to the other designers. I also had to ensure that both the developers at Capgemini and the staff at Volvo were kept in the loop and up to speed. Additionally, I held remote usability tests with mechanics based in the UK.

My role required balancing both the broader perspective and the meticulous attention to detail in pixel-perfect design.

## A Challenging Project

This project posed significant challenges from a managerial standpoint. There were many moving parts, and it was crucial to address the needs of each department before moving on to the next step. I often found myself coordinating multiple meetings to ensure that the designs met the requirements of logistics, packaging, business, developers, and all the other stakeholders involved.

Unfortunately, there was a high turnover of staff throughout the project, which made it difficult to maintain consistency. Despite these challenges, I believe I did a great job in delivering what was requested.

## Not Being Able to See It Through

After a year of working on this project, a new opportunity arose that I could not turn down. Unfortunately, this meant I was unable to see the project through to completion and never got to experience the final version of the site.

However, I learned a lot from the project and gained valuable experience.
